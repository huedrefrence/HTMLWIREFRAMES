The real cost of fast fashion nobody talks about
ET

Source: TikTok

Every fashion conversation about sustainability eventually lands on the same word: less. Buy less. Consume less. Want less. But that framing misses the actual problem — it's not how much we buy, it's how little we understand about what we're buying.

Cost per wear is the number that matters

A $200 coat worn 150 times costs $1.33 per wear. A $40 coat worn 8 times before it falls apart costs $5 per wear. The price tag tells you almost nothing about the actual cost of an item — the wear count does.

This is why "buying better" and "buying less" aren't the same instruction. You can buy more items and still buy better, if what you're buying is built to be worn for years instead of weeks.

What to actually check before buying

Look at the fabric composition first. Natural fibers with a tight weave outlast synthetic blends by years, not months. Check the stitching at stress points: underarms, crotch seams, button holes. Loose or single-stitched seams are the first thing to fail.
